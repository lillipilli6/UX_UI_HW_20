The problem

Beyond Blue is Australia’s most well-known and visited mental health organisation, focused on supporting people affected by anxiety, depression and suicide. The K10 checklist is a quick measure of a users distress levels. Currently there are 150,000 visitors to this page every month, however the bounce rate is 30% with people leaving the checklist incomplete.

The solution

Redesign the K10 checklist on the Beyond Blue wesite to increase user engagement and completion rates.

Phase 1 – Research users’ needs

Empathise

User research

To understand the problems users experienced accessing, completing and submitting the K10 checklist from the homepage of the Beyond Blue website we undertook 7 user interviews over zoom. We were also able to access insights from mental health professionals who used the form regularly with clients.

Heuristic evaluation

We examined the interface of both the home page and K10 form and judged its compliance with recognized usability principles using Jakob Nielsen's 10 general principles for interaction design.

Insights from research

Home Page

  • Hard to find suicide/crisis number
  • 24/7 contacts need to be more prominent
  • Can’t find the K10 in the navigation
  • Competing elements
  • Can’t find information about what Beyond Blue do
  • Font is too small and light in navigation

K10 Checklist

  • Text colour is hard to read and is too small
  • Need more information upfront
  • On small screen the user has to keep scrolling up to see checkbox label
  • Checklist seems scary
  • Not very inviting
