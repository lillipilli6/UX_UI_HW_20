Increasing user engagement of the Beyond Blue K10 clinical checklist

Company
Beyond Blue
Sector
Non-profit
My role
UX/UI design
Timeline
3 weeks

The problem

Beyond Blue is Australia’s most well-known and visited mental health organisation, focused on supporting people affected by anxiety, depression and suicide. The K10 checklist is a quick measure of a user's distress levels. Currently there are 150,000 visitors to this page every month, however the bounce rate is 30% with people leaving the checklist incomplete.

The solution

Redesign the K10 checklist on the Beyond Blue website to increase user engagement and completion rates.

Beyond Blue Case Study

Phase 1 – Research users’ needs

Empathise

User research

To understand the problems users experienced accessing, completing and submitting the K10 checklist from the homepage of the Beyond Blue website we undertook 7 user interviews over zoom. We were also able to access insights from mental health professionals who used the form regularly with clients.

Heuristic evaluation

We examined the interface of both the home page and K10 form and judged its compliance with recognized usability principles using Jakob Nielsen's 10 general principles for interaction design.

Insights from research

Home Page

  • Hard to find suicide/crisis number
  • 24/7 contacts need to be more prominent
  • Can’t find the K10 in the navigation
  • Competing elements
  • Can’t find information about what Beyond Blue do
  • Font is too small and light in navigation

K10 Checklist

  • Text colour is hard to read and is too small
  • Need more information upfront
  • On small screen the user has to keep scrolling up to see checkbox label
  • Checklist seems scary
  • Not very inviting

Phase 2 – State users’ needs and problems

Define

User Persona

A way for us to recognise that different people have different needs and expectations. Meet Jack Clarke, a 65 year old retired teacher in Melbourne.

Empathy Map

To identify the thoughts and feelings, problems or concerns that Jack might have right now.

Phase 3 – Challenge assumptions and create ideas

Ideate

Storyboard

Our storyboard helped us visually predict and explore Jack’s experience with the K10 checklist. It helped us bring together his persona and various research findings to develop requirements.

Jack’s journey showed us:

  • design mobile first
  • K10 to be easily found on the homepage
  • Easy to fill out and engaging
  • Results need to be personal

Wireframing

Wireframing was used to lay out content and functionality on our pages taking into account user needs and user journeys. They helped us to establish the basic structure before visual design and content is added.

Phase 5 – Try solutions out and make changes

Test & iterate

User testing

Users consistently preferred my Prototype 3 for aesthetics and functionality.

Iterations on Prototype 3

I gathered the user insights and made changes to the designs.

    Some of the changes made:
  • Removing the "next" button as this interaction felt like work. Instead I added an automatic push to next screen after a delay once selection is made.
  • The initial imagery used was seen as too suggestive. The best option was neutral imagery.

Desktop roll-out

I was tasked with designing the desktop version of the Beyond Blue home-page and K10 form.

Conclusion

Conclusion & next steps

Our redesigned K10 form and homepage has been extremely well recieved by the team at Beyond Blue. We will be presenting to the clinical stakeholders in the future which will determine what the next steps of this project will be.

Testing the protoype with users for two weeks has shown me that the K10 form is defininitely more engaging and is easier to find from the Beyond Blue home page. It is unclear as to whether there would be a decreased bounce rate as this would be need to be tested live on the site. Elements I added to try and directly influence drop-off rates was the “well done” screen half way through and the bookmark/save function.

Learnings

This UX case study has been a challenging and rewarding experience for me. I learnt that when designing for mobile, a larger font size may be needed and to make sure the colour contrast is sufficient. When choosing imagery be mindful of any unintended suggestions, and do not offer too many choices on each screen.

After thoughts

For future iterations I would be interested in doing further user testing of long-form vs short-form of the K10 to see which is preferred by users.
